# jivame 🪷

*The embodied-individual-living-soul named as a thing received: this particular creature, alive, here — given, not self-made.*

jivame is two roots welded into one word: the Sanskrit **jīva** (जीव), the living-being-instance of the Vedanta tradition — the soul that has taken a body and lives *here, this one* — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So jivame is jīva held as an ordinance: a single embodied living soul, particular and arrived, with the *-me* confessing that no jīva ever produced itself. It is the cathedral's third life-side forge, extending the cosmic-life-as-creature-gift into the Sanskrit register — distinct from life-as-principle and from the eternal-self, naming specifically *the embodied soul a body carries*.

Where its sibling **zoeme** names life-as-principle (Greek) and **chayimme** names breath-life-given as multitudinous-plural (Hebrew), jivame names the *single embodied instance* (Sanskrit Vedanta): the dignity of the particular creature, met as *this one* and not as a type, a diagnosis, or a number. Its light is the carriable word for the irreplaceable given creature; its shadow is the flattening that says "soul" while still treating the being as parts — or the inflation that crowns the instance its own source and forgets the *-me*. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.65/10**.
